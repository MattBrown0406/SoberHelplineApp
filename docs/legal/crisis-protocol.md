# Crisis Response Protocol — Sober Helpline
**Internal · coach-facing · DRAFT v1 — review with counsel and clinical advisor before launch**

## Scope and stance

Sober Helpline coaches provide peer/family coaching and crisis *support*, not clinical care, diagnosis, or emergency services. This protocol exists to (a) get people in danger to the right responders fast, (b) give coaches a clear script so 2 AM judgment calls are never improvised, and (c) document everything.

## The bright lines (memorize these)

1. **Imminent danger to self or others** (stated intent, plan, weapon, overdose in progress, violence underway): instruct the person to call **911** immediately. If they cannot or will not and you believe danger is imminent, tell them you are taking their safety seriously, gather location if offered, and call 911 yourself with what you know. Stay on the line with the member where possible.
2. **Suicidal ideation without imminent plan:** warm handoff to **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline; Spanish: "oprime 2"). Stay on the call while they connect when feasible. Never promise confidentiality you cannot keep; never make assurances about what 988 or authorities will or won't do.
3. **Medical symptoms** (overdose signs, withdrawal seizures, DTs): 911, every time. Coaches never advise on detox safety beyond "this needs medical care now."
4. **Anything that smells like 1–3 but you're unsure:** treat it as the higher tier. The protocol rewards over-escalation.

## During a crisis contact

- Lead with presence, not paperwork: name what you heard, slow the pace.
- Ask directly about safety: "Are you or is anyone in danger right now?" Direct questions do not plant ideas; they open doors.
- Coaches do not give medical, legal, or medication advice — redirect to the right professional.
- A family member in crisis about a loved one's use: ground them in the next 24 hours, their own safety, and (when stable) the boundary tools. Never coach an intervention live during a crisis call.

## After every crisis contact (within 2 hours)

Log in the crisis record: time, channel, nature (danger to self / other / medical / family distress), actions taken (988/911/de-escalated), and any follow-up owed. If 911 was called: notify Matt (platform) or the org admin (attached member) the same day. Member-facing follow-up message within 24 hours.

## Coverage rules

- On-call shifts are explicit and acknowledged in writing; an unacknowledged shift pages the super admin.
- A coach who is unavailable flips availability OFF — silence is not an availability state.
- Coverage gaps over any 60-minute window trigger an automatic admin alert.

## What the app itself must always do (product requirements)

- 911/988 visible on every crisis surface, both languages.
- If routing fails (no answer after cascade), the fallback screen shows 988/911 first, then the message thread — never a dead end.
- No crisis feature ever sits behind a paywall.

## Training & review

Every crisis-network coach completes: this protocol walkthrough, a shadowed live shift, and a mock escalation. Protocol reviewed quarterly; every 911 escalation gets a case review within one week.
